Operator: Welcome to Apple’s Q2 earnings conference call.

CEO: We are pleased with our quarterly performance and continued ecosystem expansion across global markets.

CEO: AI-related demand accelerated significantly during the quarter, particularly across enterprise productivity applications and cloud-enabled workflows.

CEO: Enterprise customers increasingly adopted Apple silicon infrastructure for machine learning and creative workloads.

CFO: Revenue grew 11% year-over-year, driven primarily by Services, Mac, and iPad segments.

CFO: Gross margins expanded due to operational efficiencies, lower logistics costs, and stronger high-margin services growth.

Analyst: Can management comment on AI infrastructure demand trends?

CEO: We continue to observe strong enterprise investment in AI-enabled devices and cloud-integrated workflows.

CEO: Demand for on-device AI capabilities increased substantially across both consumer and enterprise customers.

Analyst: How is the China market performing?

CFO: iPhone demand in China remained resilient despite macroeconomic softness and currency headwinds.

CEO: Customer engagement in Greater China improved sequentially during the quarter.

Analyst: Could you discuss supply chain dynamics?

CEO: Supply chain constraints eased considerably compared to prior quarters.

CFO: Component availability improved across semiconductor and display suppliers.

CEO: Logistics and freight costs declined meaningfully, supporting margin expansion.

Analyst: What trends are you seeing in Services revenue?

CFO: Services revenue reached an all-time high driven by App Store growth, subscriptions, cloud storage, and payment services.

CEO: Customer retention across the services ecosystem remained extremely strong.

Analyst: How are enterprise customers responding to Apple silicon adoption?

CEO: Enterprise deployment of Apple silicon accelerated globally, especially among software development and AI engineering teams.

CEO: Performance-per-watt efficiency continues to differentiate our hardware offerings.

Analyst: What are management’s expectations for future growth?

CFO: We remain optimistic regarding long-term growth opportunities in AI infrastructure, cloud integration, and enterprise productivity.

CEO: Investments in generative AI and machine learning technologies will continue increasing over the coming years.

Analyst: Can management comment on regulatory risks?

CEO: We continue engaging constructively with regulators across multiple jurisdictions.

CFO: Regulatory uncertainty remains a potential headwind for digital platform businesses globally.

Analyst: What are the current capital allocation priorities?

CFO: Capital allocation remains focused on long-term innovation, share repurchases, and strategic infrastructure investments.

CEO: We continue investing heavily in research and development across AI, silicon engineering, and cloud services.

Analyst: Are gaming and GPU workloads contributing to demand?

CEO: GPU-intensive creative and gaming workloads contributed positively to Mac demand during the quarter.

CEO: AI-assisted content generation applications also supported higher-end hardware demand.

Analyst: How is macroeconomic uncertainty affecting customer behavior?

CFO: Consumer spending patterns remain somewhat cautious in certain international markets.

CEO: However, enterprise technology spending remained relatively stable throughout the quarter.

Analyst: What are the company’s views on cloud computing growth?

CEO: Hybrid cloud adoption continues accelerating among enterprise customers globally.

CEO: Cloud-integrated software ecosystems remain an important long-term growth driver.

Operator: This concludes Apple’s Q2 earnings conference call.
